What CDLI Looks for in a Proposal

A strong proposal clearly shows that:

You understand the project you want to work on.
You have the skills or experience to complete it.
You can foresee challenges and explain how you will approach them.
You have a realistic plan and timeline.

Clear thinking and planning matter more than perfect language.

Proposal Sections

1. Identity

Provide enough information to identify yourself:

Full name
Email address
GitHub/GitLab username
Any relevant links (portfolio, LinkedIn, personal site)

Please avoid personal details such as age or unrelated background.

2. Project

Give your project a title and describe it in your own words.
Do not copy directly from the ideas list — we want to see your understanding.
Include:

A short summary of what you plan to build
Why it matters to CDLI
A brief overview of your approach


3. Interest

Explain why you want to participate in GSoC, why CDLI interests you, and why this specific project excites you.
This helps mentors understand your motivation and commitment.

4. Education and Experience

Describe your relevant background:

Degrees or coursework
Technical experience (professional, academic, open source, or personal projects)
Tools or languages you are comfortable with

Focus on experience that supports your success in this project.

5. Method (Approach)

Explain how you plan to complete the project.
A good approach section should:

Break the project into clear phases
Describe how you will handle technical challenges
Explain how you will test and validate your work

This is usually one of the most important sections.

6. Timeline

Include a week-by-week plan for the full GSoC period.
Make sure to include:

Setup and exploration
Development phases
Testing and refinement
Documentation and feedback time

If you have exams or other commitments, include them.
Example:



Weeks
Tasks




1–2
Project setup, environment, and research


3–4
Initial implementation


5–7
Core feature development


8–9
Testing and refinement


10–11
Documentation and polish


12
Final review and wrap up
